  2. Current chopping mainly occurs in

    Air blast circuit breaker of circuit breakers retain the same distinguish power irrespective of the magnitude of the electric current to be interrupted. When breaking low electric current with such breakers, powerful de-ionising effect of air blast causes. The electric current to fall abruptly to zero before the natural electric current zero is reached. This phenomenon is known as electric current chopping & result in the production of high voltage transient across the contact of the circuit breaker.

  5. Keeping in view the cost and overall effectiveness, the following circuit breaker is suited for capacitor bank switching

    The arc time constant is the least (a few microsec) in vacuum C.B as compared to other breaker types. The rapid building up of dielectric strength after final arc extinction (20kv/microsec) is unique feature of VCBs. These arc, therefore ideally suited for capacitor switching.

  6. In a short circuit test circuit breaker , time to reach the peak re-striking voltage 60 microsecond & the peak re-striking voltage 120 kV. Determine Average RRRV (Rate of rise re-striking voltage)

    Avarage RRRV = 120 KV / 60 microsecond = 2 KV / microsecond = 2 X 106 KV / sec.

  19. Main bus bar protection scheme is /are

    Differential protection is most sensible and reliable method & fault bus protection gives earth fault protection of bus bar. Busbars in the generating stations & sub-stations form important link between the incoming & outgoing circuits. If a fault-current on a busbar, considerable damage and disruption of supply will occur unless some form of quick-acting automatic protection is provided to isolate the faulty busbar. The standard of construction for busbars has been very high, with the result that bus-faults are extremely rare. However, the possibility of damage & service interruption from even a rare bus fault is so great that more attention is now given to this form of protection. Improved relaying methods have been developed, reducing the possibility of incorrect operation.
