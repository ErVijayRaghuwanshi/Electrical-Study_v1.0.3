Discuss about Substation Grounding.

Electrical substations comprises of various costly electrical equipments, and they also deals with very high voltage level. Therefore primary protection for substations must to be ensured in order to increase the level of safety for operators as well as costly equipments. The reliability is quite ensured if the substation provides built in internal grounding, although it is helpful for connected consumers too. In general, Earthing or grounding is a process in which an electrical connection is made in between the live parts of apparatus and the earth. The objective of grounding is to transfer the huge fault current toward the earth, and safeguard the operators and equipments from the effects of surge. The foundation of substation must be grounded properly, the concrete base is situated at the soil level. A new technology is applied in direct ground for concrete and steel foundation, termed as “Ufer”. Here the steel structure itself behaves like an earth electrode. So each steel foundation (like towers, transformer base with nuts and bolts) is subjected to built in earthing which further increases the level of safety. The over current is transferred to the earth locally via these Ufer earthing foundation.

In substations, grounding is provided in various means. Equipments can be surrounded with metallic fences which are actually grounded. And for delta connected network, a virtual neutral point is required. That is why a grounding transformer is installed which is basically an interconnected star winding transformer and it assemble all the phases. After neutralization a grounding transformer connects them all and combine with earthing electrode. Power transformer is the most costly equipment in a substation, so LA is installed near every transformer. LA is full form of lighting arrestor, which is actually a grounded path which goes through some Zinc Oxide cubes. In normal operating conditions, these cubes behaves like an insulator but when the high amplitude fault current or surge current starts flowing through the circuit, they become conductor and transfer the fault current towards earth. Additionally local earthing is provided with each transformer for better safety.
