What are the Types of ADC ?

Analog to Digital conversion (ADC) and Digital to Analog conversion techniques acts as an interface between a completely digital system like a computer and the analog world. Most physical values are analog in nature for example â€“ temperature, pressure, light intensity, rotational speed, etc. Analog to Digital converter takes an analog input voltage and after a certain amount of time produces a digital output code which represents analog input. This conversion method is more complex and time consuming than DAC process. There are quite a few types of ADC namely - digital ramp A/D converter, successive approximation A/D converter, single slop A/D converter, Dual slope A/D converter, counting ADC, parallel comparator ADC. Let us discuss each of these methods in brief.

Digital Ramp A/D converter: This is also called as stair step ramp or counter method, because waveform is a step-by-step ramp (staircase). It consists of counter, DAC, analog comparator and a control AND gate. Conversion time for this type of ADC is much more and thus it is used for low speed applications.

Successive Approximation A/D converter: It is most widely used and has more complex circuitry than the digital ramp A/D converter but a much shorter conversion time. Here the conversion time is not dependent on the values of the analog input. This type is used mainly in data acquisition applications and it performs very high speed operation.

Single slop A/D converter: It has the advantage of relatively low cost because it does not require precision components like DAC or VCO. It uses linear ramp generator to produce a constant slope reference voltage.

Dual slope A/D converter: This uses two ramp generators- one is variable slope ramp and other is fixed slope ramp. This converter possesses slowest conversion time. The basic operation includes linear charging and discharging of capacitor. Advantages of this type of converter are - low cost, low sensitivity to noise, etc.
