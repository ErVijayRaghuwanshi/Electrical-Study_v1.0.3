What is Analog to Digital Converter or ADC converter?

Analog to digital converter is a common name in electronics world. The analog signals are the continuous flow of data and the representation is also continuous modules. But the digital signals are truncated signals which are preferably termed as some small events, and they have only high state and low state. Think about the computer, it operates on binary signal which handles the digital signal processing. But the mouse or Keyboard is provides their output as analog signal waveform. So there must a middle state operation which converts the analog signal into digital, in order to bring it into understandable language for computers. So the analog to digital converter or simply A to D converter is very important in many electronic applications.

Ideal ADC is able to convert the full range analog signal in bitwise digital signal, with keeping the noise level very low. Low noise or noiseless operation of analog to digital converter is very much necessary as noises are harmful for signal processing. So how it operates ? A typical analog to digital converter captures the continuous analog signal with a definite time interval, which is known as the sampling time. Those collected sampled data bits together forms the event based digital signal. This digital signal is then transmitted in other components for further processing. Handling and operation with the digital signals is very easy and accurate, that is why they are used for processing. Analog to digital converters are used in microcomputers, minicomputers, modem, signal processing devices and in many other fields.
