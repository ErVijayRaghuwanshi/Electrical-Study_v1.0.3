What is Power Factor?

In alternating current (A.C) circuit there is a phase difference between voltage and current. This phase difference is represented by Φ. The cosine of angle between voltage and current in AC circuit is known as power factor. Power factor of the circuit is termed as cos Φ. Power factor is also of three types namely â€“ unity, lagging and leading power factor. Power factor is lagging or leading based on inductive or capacitive circuits. If current lags voltage then the power factor is lagging and it happens in case of inductive circuit and current leads voltage then the power factor is leading. These circuits with different power factor type can be expressed with phasor diagrams. In AC circuits the power consumed depends upon power factor. The load current varies inversely with the power factor of the circuit. Thus low power factor especially less than unity results in some disadvantages. These are:
i) Large KVA rating of electrical equipments because KVA = KW / cos Φ,
ii) Greater conductor size,
iii) copper losses are more,
iv) Low voltage regulation.
Low power factor can be improved by various methods. One common thing which is used for power factor improvement is capacitor bank. Static capacitors, synchronous condensers and phase advancers are some of the equipments which helps in improving power factor.
