What are the uses of the thermocouples?

A thermocouple is nothing but a junction point of two dissimilar conductors. A certain amount of voltage is generated at the junction point of those conductors, whenever a temperature difference is occurred in between the junction point & any other parts of those conductors.

As they are self powered device so they are very economical. Thermocouples are used in various fields.

• Thermocouples are widely used in temperature measurement as they are cheap, interchangeable, are made with available connectors, and can measure a wide range of temperatures, up to 2300 °C.

• They also used as a temperature sensor. That is they are used to sense the temperature difference.

• An interesting application of the thermocouple is, it often used to convert a temperature difference into electricity. More easily, they are used to convert heat power into electric power.
