What are the types of transmission line towers?

The supporting structures for overhead transmission line and conductors are various types of poles and towers. These structures are called supports. There are different types of line supports:

• Wooden poles :

  1.   made up of sal
  2.  low mechanical strength
  3.   cannot be used for high voltages
  4.   used in rural areas

• Steel towers :

  1.   High mechanical strength
  2.    Life span longer
  3.    Types :- i) rail poles, ii) tubular poles, iii) rolled steel poles
  4.   Used in cities mainly

• R.C.C poles ( Reinforced concrete) poles.

• Lattice steel towers.

A transmission towers is a large structure which is designed with the following parts:

  1.   Peak : it is the part of the tower above cross arms
  2.   Cross arm : the structure that provides support to the insulators and it is the distance of the conductor from tower. We can improve string efficiency by using longer cross arms.
  3.   Cage : is the portion of the towers in between peak and tower body
  4.  Tower body : the part of the tower from below the cross arm upto bottom/ground level of the transmission line tower.
