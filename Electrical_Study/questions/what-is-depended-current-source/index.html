What is Depended Current Source?

Current sources are those electronic energy circuits where current can be absorbed or delivered. So it acts as both source and sink. Current source are of two types mainly - dependent current source and independent current source. Independent current source circuit acts as current sink. Electric current is delivered at constant rate. Here the current is independent of the voltage across it. Dependent current source circuit is that which delivers or absorbs current which is proportional to other voltage or other current in the circuit. The proportionality constant may have a dimension or it may be dimensionless.

The main feature of dependent source is that here current taken varies with other voltage and current of the circuit as we have already mentioned. So the current source can be controlled. We have voltage controlled current source (VCCS) as well as current controlled current source (CCCS). Apart from dependent current source there is also dependent voltage source. Dependent sources are very useful in modeling various electronic devices or circuits. They are used in modeling the nature of amplifier or transistors. BJT (bi polar junction transistor) can be used as dependent current source. Dependent current source can be connected to a voltage source without any problem and it is used widely used circuitry.
