What is DC motor working principle?

Direct current motors are widely used to provide motion in a variety of products. In any DC motor, the operation is based on simple electromagnetism theory.

As you know, two opposite polarities attract each other, but similar polarities repel each other. This simple attraction- repulsion phenomenon is utilized to convert the electrical energy to the mechanical energy by means of shaft rotation. In any DC motor, a closed current-carrying conductor, called armature is placed in between an external magnetic field. In small DC motor the external magnetic field is created by placing permanent magnates but in large DC motor this field is provided by a field coil. Thus, whenever a current is passing through the armature coil, it generates a magnetic field.

As, the armature is placed in between an external magnetic field, it will experience a force which is proportional.

• to the current in the conductor,

• to the strength of the external magnetic field.

The construction of a DC motor :

The internal configuration of any DC motor is designed to utilize the magnetic interaction between a current-carrying conductor(armature) and an external magnetic field to create rotational motion.
