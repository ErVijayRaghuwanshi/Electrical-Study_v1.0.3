What is triangular wave?

A signal is represented in the form of waves. Waveform is the shape and form of a signal. Waves do propagate from one place to another through a medium. Sometimes they do not require any medium. Electromagnetic wave is of this kind. Other types of waves are light wave, sound wave, etc. Waveforms may be periodic or aperiodic in nature. Periodic waveform includes sine waves, triangular wave, square wave, saw tooth wave.

Triangular wave is thus a periodic waveform which contains odd harmonics. Triangular waveform is named so due to its shape. Triangular waveform is non-sinusoidal in nature and it is also a continuous real function. Due to its odd symmetry only odd harmonics are present in this kind of waveform. Triangular function is also known as the tent or hat function. Fourier series is very important in formulating the equation of triangular wave. The basic equation of triangular wave form is given as, V = 2 / π Vpk θ where θ varies from zero to ninety degree and Vpk is the peak voltage.

Triangular waveform can be generated from triangular wave generator. The generator circuit consists of o-amp, capacitors and resistors. This kind of wave generator is obtained by cascading square wave generator and an integrator. Triangular waveforms have huge application in automotive industry. It can be also used to test linearity of analog system. Triangular waveforms acts as a reference wave in producing pulse width modulation (PWM).
