Measurement of Capacitance

Measurement of capacitance is a very easy process is it is accomplished with proper procedure. Capacitor is a device which consists of a pair of parallel plates separated by a dielectric medium between them and it is capable of storing electrical charge. Capacitance is simply a measure of electric energy stored in a capacitor. SI unit of capacitance is called as Farad. However capacitance is also measured in smaller units like micro farads, mili farads, pico farads, etc.

If the two plates are charged with +q and –q charge and the voltage between them is V volts then capacitance is given by C = q / V. For measuring the capacitance we need proceed through the following steps. These are – at first we need to find out the charge in Coulomb. Finding out charge directly is a bit difficult so we find out current and time of flow of current to find out charge which is also given as ampere- second. Next step involves measurement of voltage. It can be done by a voltmeter or a multimeter with voltage setting on it. Finally we divide the charge in coulomb with the potential / voltage value in volts and we get capacitance C in farad. Different bridges like De Sauty’s bridge and Schering’s bridge (derived from Wheatstone bridge) also measures unknown capacitances in electronic circuits.
