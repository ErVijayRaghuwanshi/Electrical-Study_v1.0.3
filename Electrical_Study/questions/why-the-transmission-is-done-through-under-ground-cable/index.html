Why the transmission is done through under ground cable?

There are two types of cabling system:

  1. Overhead cable system.
  2. Underground cable system.

Both of these have several advantages and disadvantages. Here we are discussing about the underground lines. So, first let us see some of the main disadvantages of underground cables.

The main disadvantage of underground cable is its costing. Huge primary expense is required to produce and install underground cables, where as it is very much less for overhead lines.

Another disadvantage of these cables is that, if any fault occurs in underground cables it is quite difficult to trace and repair.

Even though for all of these underground cables also have many advantages for which these are widely used. The main is that these cables are less vulnerable to natural forces (calamities) i.e. wind, ice etc.

Where there is more population, underground cables are preferred because of the safety factor, which is less for overhead cables and underground cables saves the space also.

There is always a risk factor involved with the overhead lines as the towers may collapse, which is not present for the underground lines.
