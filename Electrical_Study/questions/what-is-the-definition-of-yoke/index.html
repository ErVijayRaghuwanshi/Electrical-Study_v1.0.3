What is the definition of yoke?

A DC (direct current) machine consists of four main parts. These are – Field systems, armature, commutator and brushes. The field system is used to create a uniform magnetic field, within which armature rotates. Electromagnets are preferred in comparison with permanent magnets. Field magnets consist of four parts:

  1.   Yoke / frame
  2.   Pole cores
  3.   Pole shoe
  4.   Magnetizing coils

Yoke or frame is the important part of dc machine. Usually cylindrical yoke is used which acts as a frame of the machine. This frame carries the flux produced by the poles. Since the magnetic field is stationary, laminated yoke is not used in case of normal machines. We use cast iron yokes in case of small machines. Yoke of large machines are made up of fabricated steel due to its high permeability. In case of small machines the cheapness is the main consideration not the weight but in large machines, weight is the main issue. As permeability of cast steel is about twice of cast iron the weight of cast iron, weight of cast steel needed is half of the cast iron for same reluctance. Yoke should possess high mechanical strength and high permeability. In small machines we use yoke which is one piece but in large machines we use split yoke. The two halves are bolted together. So yoke serves following main purposes:

  1. It supports pole cores.
  2. Acts as protective covering of machine.
  3. It forms a part of magnetic circuit.
