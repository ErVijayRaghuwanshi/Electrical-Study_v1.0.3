What are magnetic lines of force ?

Actually, magnetic lines of force are the imaginary lines which are drawn to show the pattern of magnetic field of a magnet. The magnetic lines of force can travel through any object; it may be magnetic material or may be non-magnetic material.

We know that, any magnet has of two poles, namely North Pole & South Pole. Conventionally, these lines of force start their journey from the North pole & travel along the surrounding space of the magnet & ultimately enter in the South pole. After reaching to the South pole they further proceed towards the North pole inside the magnet. In this way, the magnetic lines of force complete their journey by forming a closed loop. So, an important characteristic of the magnetic lines of force is, these lines are continuous & construct a closed loop. The another property of these lines is, they never overlaps each other because any two parallel magnetic lines of force are repelled by each other if they are travelling in the same direction.

Think about a simple rubber band. If you stretch the band with your fingers then the band will try to pull your fingers. Now, compare magnetic lines of force with the rubber band. Like the rubber band the magnetic lines of force existing in between two different poles (such as N & S) cause the poles to be pulled.
