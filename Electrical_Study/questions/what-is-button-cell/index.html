What is button cell ?

Button cells are very small sized battery cells specially used in calculators, cameras, watches, computer memories and in many other sophisticated digital appliances. The button cells are mainly silver oxide and mercury type.

In mercuric oxide button cell, a saturated solution of potassium hydroxide and zinc oxide is used as electrolyte.That is why this cell is classified as alkaline type.The cathode is made of either a cylinder of perforated zinc foil or a sintered zinc-powder cylinder fastened to the button-top of the cell and it is insulated from the bottom casing.The anode of the cell is formed by a coating of mercury oxide mixed with graphite on nickel plated steel or stainless steel casing of the cell. In anode graphite is used to improve conductivity of the anode.Emf range of this cell is about 1.2 to 1.3 V. This cell has high energy content and long self â€“ life and free from local action.

The silver oxide cell is similar to mercuric oxide cell but only difference is that here silver oxide mixed with graphite is used for anode instead of mercury oxide mixed with graphite.The cell can deliver constant emf of 1.5 V consistently throughout its long service life.Both mercuric oxide and silver oxide cells are used only where very tiny current is required. So through very narrow leakage path the battery may be discharged. Hence, special care to be taken during handling these battery cells. Even these cells should not be touched by naked hand since this will leave a film of perspiration on the cells which are sufficient enough to reduce its service life by providing leakage path through this film.
