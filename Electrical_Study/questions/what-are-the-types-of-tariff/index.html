What are the types of tariff?

The generating stations generate electrical energy. This energy is transmitted to the intermediate substations and finally distributed to a large number of distributors. Consumers have to pay some amount for consuming the electrical energy. The rate at which electrical energy is sold by the electrical companies is called tariff. Tariff aims at recovering the production cost, operation and maintenance cost and also earns desired amount of profit. All consumers do not have the same tariff for consuming electrical energy. It depends upon the magnitude of electrical energy consumption and also the load condition of the user. There are several types of tariff. Most common ones are:

  1. Simple tariff - Rate of per unit of energy consumed is fixed in this case. It does not vary with the increase or decrease of Flat rate tariff – In this type different consumers have different rate of per unit of electricity consumption. Consumers are classified based upon their consumption and also the load.
  2. Block rate tariff – Energy consumption is divided into blocks. First block of energy units are charges maximum then the rates reduces progressively. In this practice more electrical consumption gives some concession to the users.
  3. Two part tariff - When the rate of electrical energy is charged on the basis of maximum demand of the consumer and the units consumed, it is called a two part tariff. Here total charge is split into two components – fixed charge and running charges.
  4. Maximum demand tariff – It is similar to two part tariff. The only difference is that maximum demand is measured by installing maximum demand meter.
  5. Power factor (p.f) tariff – The tariff in which p.f of the consumer is taken into account.
  6. Three part tariff – Total charge here is splitted into three parts – fixed, semi fixed and running charges. Applied for big consumers.
