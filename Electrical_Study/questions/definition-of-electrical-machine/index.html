Definition of electrical machine

Electricity is produced in power plants, transmitted through overhead or underground cables and ultimately distributed among the customers. The generation process includes some operation with moving parts. Here mainly mechanical power is converted into electrical power, and the mechanical power is received from wind, water, heat etc. natural resources. Almost everywhere a turbine is made to rotate and a shaft is connected with this turbine. This mechanical power generates electric power. For transmission purpose power transformers are used which step up or step down voltage and in case of distribution, distribution transformers are used. Where these electric power is utilized, i.e. at home, factories, electrical energy is converted into either light, heat or mechanical energy. Where the electric power is converted into mechanical energy, there also some moving parts are engaged. Now coming to the main topic i.e. an electric machine is any equipment in which either input or output or both are electrical energy. Now depending on the above mentioned criteria electrical machine can be divided into three parts.

  1. Generator - the output is electrical.
  2. Motor - the input is electrical.
  3. Transformer - both output and input are electrical.

Motor and generator can be easily termed as machine but why we are considering transformer as an electrical machine as it does not have any rotating parts. The answer lies in the fact that in transformers electromagnetic phenomenon is utilized. The main operating principle of an electrical machine is totally depended on the phenomenon of electromagnetism. Now the electric machines have some classification within themselves, which are:

Generator-

  1. AC generator
  2. DC generator

Motor

  1. AC motor
  2. DC motor

From another point of view the machines can be divides as

  1. Permanent magnet machines
  2. Brushed machine
  3. Induction machines
  4. Reluctance machines
  5. Electrostatic machines.
