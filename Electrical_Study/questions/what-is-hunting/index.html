What is hunting?

When a synchronous motor runs in slightly less or more than the synchronous speed an unbalanced condition arises in the motor. This particular phenomenon of a synchronous machine is known as hunting of synchronous machine. Normally when a synchronous motor is running in normal condition, the speed of the rotor is same as the synchronous speed or the speed of the rotating flux, i.e. the relative speed between the rotor and the stator field is zero. Now in certain circumstances this relative speed changes, sometimes in negative or sometimes in positive quantity. Then the rotor of the motor starts to oscillate and this is referred as hunting . The main causes of hunting are:

  1. Sudden change in the load connected with the motor.
  2. Radical change in the field current.
  3. A fault in the system.
  4. Load with harmonic torques.

Consequences of hunting are-

  1. Huge mechanical stress and fatigue is applied on the rotor.
  2. Huge surge flows through the network.
  3. Machine loss is increased and temperature rises.

To overcome the problem of hunting various steps are taken, these are

  1. Using of the flywheel.
  2. By employing damper windings.

Basically, damper windings are the main equipment which are widely used to overcome the problem of hunting.
