Explain the classification of lines based on their length of transmission

Generally for transmission purpose, transmission lines are used. Now when two parallel transmission lines are laid, there are four parameters comes into the calculation, and they are series resistance, inductance, capacitance and conductance. Depending upon the length of the transmission lines these parameters have their influence on the circuit and depending on that, the parameters are taken into account.

So, the transmission lines are divided in three types:

  1. Short transmission line:- Any transmission line whose length is below 80 km is termed as short transmission line. For short transmission lines shunt capacitance is not considered. All the series resistances and inductances are assumed to be lumped together.
  2. Medium length transmission lines:- Transmission line whose length is between 80 km to 160 km are termed as medium length transmission lines. Here, two models are present in this category. One is nominal-T network. Here the shunt capacitance is assumed to be concentrated in the middle of the line and the series impedance at each side of the line. And the other model is nominal-π network. Here, the shunt capacitance is assumed to be at two sides of the series impedance.
  3. Long transmission line :- Transmission lines whose lengths are more than 160 km are termed as long transmission line. For long transmission line the parameter are not assumed to be distributed uniformly. This results in more accurate calculation.
