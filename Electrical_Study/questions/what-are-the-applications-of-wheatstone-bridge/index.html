What are the applications of Wheatstone Bridge?

We come to know about Wheatstone bridge circuits while studying electrical circuits. This circuit is very useful in solving electrical measurement problems. It was first developed by Samuel Christie and later it was designed by Sir Wheatstone in the year of 1843. Wheatstone bridge has four arms consisting of resistances, inductances or even capacitance. Generally this bridge measures the unknown electrical resistance by balancing the other arms. A basic Wheatstone bridge consists of a voltage source and also a galvanometer. Wheatstone bridge can also measures even small value of resistance change in strain gauge. We will discuss here regarding the applications of this bridge. Wheatstone bridge is a special circuit which has a lot of applications on different electrical and electronic measurement phenomenon.

There are many other bridges which have been developed from the main Wheatstone bridge. Kelvin bridge, Maxwell bridge, Wien's bridge and others are some examples. Kelvin bridge is used to measure low resistance while Maxwell bridge measures inductances. Sometimes Wheatstone bridge can indirectly measures force, pressure, temperature, etc by finding out the unknown resistances. This is possible because the resistance measurement was related with these physical factors. So this bridge circuit is used across amplifiers also. In one application one arm of the bridge is replace by a thermistor whose resistance varies with temperature changes. This is applicable for measuring small changes in temperature. As temperature rises resistance of the thermistor decreases. Here the bridge output is connected to the amplifier across which an alarm is connected. Under balanced condition the voltage across two series resistance arms are equal. Output potential is zero. But when the temperature drops the temperature at both series resistors varies and in this way potential difference is created. This causes the alarm to get activated.
