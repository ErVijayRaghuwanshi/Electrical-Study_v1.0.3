Definition of magnetic field strength.

Initially, we should know the concept of a magnetic field. A magnet always creates an invisible field around it which is termed as the magnetic field. This magnetic field is responsible to attract other magnetic materials like iron, nickel or cobalt towards the magnet. Actually, magnetic field strength is a measure of the intensity of magnetic field around a magnet.

Letâ€™s make it simpler, we know that the magnetic lines of force are the imaginary lines which are drawn to show the pattern of magnetic field of a magnet. The total number of magnetic lines of force penetrating a unit area is called the magnetic flux. The magnetic field strength is depended on the density of those magnetic field lines. So, we can say that more number of magnetic lines of force means more magnetic field strength and vice versa.

So, in the other words we can say that, magnetic field strength is nothing but the amount of magnetizing force which depends on the length of conductor and the amount of electrical current passing through the conductor. Magnetic field strength is a vector quantity as it consists of magnitude & direction. The magnitude is nothing but the strength of that magnetic field at a given point & the direction is towards the magnetic field in that point.

Typically, magnetic field strength is denoted by H, and the unit of is amperes per meter.
