What is silicon diode?

In the world of electronics diode is one of the important components. It is a two terminal semiconductor electronic device. There are two polarities – positive and the negative. Silicon diode helps in current flow in one direction only. Apart from silicon (Si) some other substances called doping agents (boron, gallium) are added to it increase its conductivity. The diode has two junction p and n. The positive side of the diode is made up of p type silicon and it is called the anode. Cathode is the name given to the negative side which is made up of n type silicon. Silicon diode is forward bias with knee voltage of 0.7 volt. When it reaches 0.7 volts it starts conducting current. Forward resistance of diode is kept to a small value. The breakdown voltage of silicon diode is equal to or above 50 volts. Silicon diodes are made in different pattern which has varying parameters. The current carrying capacity varies from milliamps to several amps. Silicon diode finds application in different electronic devices which are – radio, computer, thermal sensor, AC/DC power supplies. Silicon diodes are used as voltage regulators, signal rectifiers, oscillators and modulators or demodulators.
