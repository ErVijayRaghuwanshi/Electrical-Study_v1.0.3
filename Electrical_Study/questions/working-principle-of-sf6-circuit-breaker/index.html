Working principle of SF6 circuit breaker

A circuit breaker is made to operate mainly on two occasions. One is during the maintenance operation and the other is during any abnormal condition in the power system.

The basic operation of a SF6 circuit breaker is to quench the arc formed when the two contacts start to move away from each other.

Looking into the basic operation of a SF6 circuit breaker we can see from the figure that there is a cylinder and piston arrangement. In this case the piston is fixed and the cylinder is movable. The SF6 gas is kept inside the cylinder and it is compressed. When the contacts move away from each other and the arc is formed the SF6 gas which is compressed flows through the nozzle and around the arc in axial direction. Now when the arc reaches current zero, due to heat convection and increase in the dielectric strength of the medium gradually the arc radius reduces and finally quenched. Now during this operation small amount of SF6 gas produce some other forms of fluorides. Activated alumina is kept inside the chamber as an absorber for the by products produced.
