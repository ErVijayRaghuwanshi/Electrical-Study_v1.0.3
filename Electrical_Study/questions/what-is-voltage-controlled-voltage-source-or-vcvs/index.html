What is voltage controlled voltage source or VCVS

There are several kinds of energy sources which are used for different types applications. A voltage controlled voltage source is also termed as VCVS. It is simply a special kind of voltage source, where the output voltage is controlled by another voltage source. So, there is main voltage source associated with a VCVS circuit, which is termed as controller voltage source which controls the voltage level of the VCVS along with a series resistor. Suppose, the voltage of controller voltage source is V volt. Then the voltage output of the voltage controlled voltage source will be gV volt, where g is the constant. This g is actually the portionality constant, which generates a connection in between the dependent and independent variables.

Voltage controlled voltage source is actually a depended voltage source, where the output voltage is controlled by the controller voltage source and the series resistor. Here the output voltage is easily controlled by changing the voltage of the controller voltage source and manipulating the resistor. Sometime change in the controller voltage is achieved by simply vary the resistor connected with the VCVS circuit. As the voltage of the circuit is depended on the circuit resistance, so by changing the circuit resistance the output voltage also tends to change in a certain level. VCVS is used in resistance control circuits, power amplifier circuits and circuits where change of parameter is required.
