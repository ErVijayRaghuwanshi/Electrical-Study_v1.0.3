What is Varactor Diode?

A varactor diode is also known as varicap diode. It is actually a special type of diode which is required for special applications. Varactor diode has the ability to change the capacitance of itself. Initially we have to know the basic principle of a capacitor. If the distance of the plates (charged electrode plates) are increased, then automatically the capacitance reduced. On the other hand, if the distance of those plates are reduced then the capacitance get increased. This simple principle is applied into the varactor diode. Like any other diodes, varactor diodes are also consist of P region and N region, more specifically P layer and N layer. A neutral space is also provided to separate those two regions from contacting with each other.

The region of neutral space or the junction barrier is not constant all the time, in fact it totally depends upon the biasing level. If reverse bias is applied to a varactor diode, then the neutral space in between P and N layer is increased. In this way, the junction barrier also increased. That is, the distance between P and N layer increased. So, if this phenomenon is compared with a capacitor, then the capacitance of the varactor is increased. The capacitance of varactor diode depends upon the netral zone or barrier space, which is further controlled by varying the voltage level of reverse bias to the varactor. Almost all diodes have the capability to change their capacitance by changing the reverse bias voltage, but varactor diodes are specially manufactured for this purpose. With the help of varactor diode, the capacitance is varied into a wide range. Varactor diodes are used in signal processing and signal modulation purposes.
