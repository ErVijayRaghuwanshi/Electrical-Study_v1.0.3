What is the magnetic pole strength ?

A magnetic pole simply refers to the part of a magnet where the magnetic strength is largest. Generally, there are two poles present in any type of magnet namely North pole & South pole. When two opposite poles ( like N & S) come nearer, then a force is exerted by both poles & they apply this force on each other.


In general, strength of a pole is a physical quantity which is used to measure the strength of the pole of a magnet. Sometime the pole may be considered as a hypothetical unit North or South pole. Consider a simple iron core. If you wound some turns of wires around it and then apply some potential difference then the iron core will behave like a magnet. So, the above said two poles will also develop. Magnetic pole strength is denoted by p & according to the formulae, if there is an infinitely long wire & the current passing through it is I, then the magnetic pole strength is: p = W / I

where, W is the work done to develop a magnetic pole, which has been turned around the wire in a closed loop.
