What is load resistance?

Load is an important terminology in electrical engineering field. In an electrical circuit if it has an output terminal then the circuit connected to that terminal is the load. Moreover it is called the electrical load.

Performance of the circuit is affected by the load. Load may be resistive, inductive or capacitive. It may be also a combination of any two from these. If we think in terms of bigger perspective then load in an electrical network is the total power demand in KW by the consumers. If there is no load we say it open circuited terminals. Voltage drop will take place if the load impedance is not much higher than the value of power supply impedance. Electrical resistance is something that opposes electrons flow that is flow of electric current.

Stability and sensitivity in the active devices such as amplifiers, transmitters, receivers, photo detectors can be adjusted by the load resistance. Impedances of the passive devices like transmission lines, etc can also be adjusted. The voltage that we get across load resistance is called as load voltage or output voltage. Current that flows through the load resistance is the load current. Load resistance is denoted by RL. Load resistance may be variable also.
