  1. A 60 W bulb in series with a room heater is connected across the mains. If the 60 W bulb is replaced by 100 W bulb

    The wattage rating of any electrical component (bulb) is inversely proportional to its resistance. Hence 100 W bulb has smaller resistance than 60 W bulb. So if 60 W bulb is replaced by 100 W bulb in the above case then, the overall resistance of the series combination of heater and bulb is reduced hence electric current increases accordingly. Therefore the output of the heater is increased as the electric current through it increases.

  2. Filaments of electric bulbs are generally made of

    Tungsten has the highest melting point of all the non-alloyed metals and the second highest of all the elements after carbon. When electric current flows through high resistive tungsten filament, it will not melt but glow. That is why filaments of electric bulbs are generally made of tungsten.

  4. The ratio of resistances of a 100 W, 220 V lamp to that of a 100 W, 110 V lamp will be at the respective voltages

    For 100 W, 220 V lamp, I220 = 100 / 220 A = 0.4545 A and R220 = 220 / 0.4545 = 484.0 Ω.
    For 100 W, 110 V lamp, I110 = 100 / 110 A = 0.9090 A and R110 = 110 / 0.9090 Ω = 121.0 Ω.
    Now, R220 / R110 = 484.0 / 121.0 = 4.

    R40 > R60 ⇒ I × R40 > I × R60 ⇒ I2 × R40 > I2R60.

  18. Two heaters, rated at 1000 W, 250 V each are connected in series across a 250 V, 50 Hz ac mains. The total power drown from the supply will be

    P = 1000 W, V= 250 V

    In series connection equivalent resistance will be double = Req = 62.5 + 62.5 Ω
    ⇒ Req = 125 Ω
    Now current will be ( 250 / 125 ) A = 2 A.
    And the power consumption will be = Pseries = 2 × (22 × 62.5) W = 500 W.
