  1. In case of a D.C shunt motor under no load condition the air gap flux

    In shunt motor, Ish = V / Rsh. If V is constant Ish will also be a constant. Hence the flux is constant.

  2. In DC series motor the speed is ______________ proportional to the armature current.

    In dc series motor,the flux φ is produced by the armature electric current flowing in the field winding and so φ is directly proportional to Ia. Now since φ is inversely proportional to speed.
    ∴ Ia is also inversely proportional to speed .

  3. A compound motor has

    In compound motor having both series and shunt field winding that is why it is called compound motor. It may be a short shunt compound or long shunt compound motor.

  4. A dc motor with armature resistance of 0.5 ohms connected directly to a 230 Volt supply. If the armature is stationary then the electric current flowing through armature is

    As the armature is stationary so the back e.m.f E is zero. The initial armature electric current Ias is given by Ias = ( V − 0 ) / Ra i.e. V / Ra. Armature resistance is 0.5 ohm and voltage is 230 V therefore Ias = V / Ra = 230 / 0.5 = 460 A.

  5. A shunt motor of 250 V having armature resistance 0.5 Ohm and no-load electric current is 2 A. When loaded and taking a armature electric current of 20 A. The speed is 1000 r.p.m. The approximate no load speed will be

    We know E0 = V − Ia0.Ra i.e 250 − 2 × 0.5 = 249 V,
    And E1 = V − Ia1.Ra i.e. 250 − 20 × 0.5 = 240 V.
    Now, N1 / N0 = E1 / E0φ0 / φ1
    For shunt motor, φ0 = φ1
    Therefore, N0 = E0 / E1N1
    i.e. ( 249 × 1000) / 240 = 1037.5 r.p.m.

  6. The armature of a DC motor is laminated to reduce

    Thinner the lamination, greater is the resistance offered to the induced emf smaller the electric current and hence lesser the I2R loss.

  7. A 4 pole lap wound DC shunt motor rotates at the speed of 1500 rpm, has a flux of 0.4 mWb and the total number of conductors are 1000. What is the value of emf?

    Given N = 1500 rpm, f = 0.4 mWb, Z = 1000, P = 4, and A = 4
    Therefore, Eb = NfPZ /60A
    = 1500 × 0.4 × 4 × 1000 × 10 − 3 / 60 × 4
    = 60 / 6 = 10 volts.

  8. Which of the following motors one will choose to drive paper machine?

    Separately excited motor always obtains accurate speed and most suitable for applications requiring speed variation from very low value to high value, like its required in a paper mill.

  9. In electric locomotive which of the following motor are used ?

    In DC series motor starting torque is very high up to 500 % resulting maximum momentary operation of 400 % thus it is employed in locomotive.

  10. A 250 V DC shunt motor having an armature resistance of 0.5 Ω and a field resistance of 250 Ω. The armature electric current draws 20 A. What is the back e.m.f of the motor?

    Given: V = 250 Volts, Ra = 0.5 Ohm, Rsh = 250 Ohm
    Shunt field current, Ish = V / Rsh = 250 / 250 = 1 A
    Armature current (Ia)= 20 Ohm
    Back e.m.f = V − IaRa = 250 − 20 × 0.5 = 240 V

  11. On what factors the speed of dc motor depends?

    Speed N proportional to Eb / φ
    N proportional to V - IaRa / φ.

  12. The speed of a motor falls from 1100 r.p.m at no-load to 1050 r.p.m at rated load. The speed regulation of motor is

    (No load sped − Rated speed) / Full load speed
    No load speed = 1100 rpm
    Full load speed = 1000 rpm
    ∴ % of Regulation = (1100 − 1050) / 1050 = 4.76%.

  13. Swinburne's test is applicable to

    Swineburne’s test is applicable to those machine in which the flux is practically constant, that is shunt machine.

  14. Which of these types of motor is used in elevators?

    In DC compound motor having high starting torque up to 450 % depending upon the degree of compounding. Speed regulation is varying up to 25 ~ 30 %. This is why those motors are used in elevators .

  15. A 4 pole 250 V wave connected shunt motor gives 10 KW when running at 1000 rpm and drawing armature and field electric current 60 A and 1 A respectively. Its armature resistance is 0.2 Ω Assuming drop of 1 V per brush. The total torque will be

    E = V − IaRa − Brush drop = 250 − 60 × 0.2 − 2 × 1 = 236 V
    We know, EIa = 2πNτ / 60
    ⇒ τ = 60EIa / 2πN = 60 × 236 × 60 / 2π × 100 = 133.22 Nm.

  16. Two identical loss less series motors connected in series across a dc supply voltage, runs at speed of N1 and N2, then ratio of their output power is

    Ia1 = Ia2, Ea1 ∝ N1, Ea2 ∝ N2.
    ∴ P1 / P2 = Ea1Ia1 / Ea2Ia2 = N1 / N2.

  17. The dc motor which can provide zero speed regulation at full load without any controller, is

    Differential compound dc motor has constant speed characteristics.

  18. Which machines are mostly gear motors?

    Because the low speed motors are much more expensive than higher speed motors of the same KW output rates.

  19. Which type of motors are preferred in lifts?

    AC slip ring or DC compound motors are prefer for lifts. Shunt type commutator motors are preferred in case of single phase installation. The latest lift designs use 3-phase induction motors with variable frequency drive electronic controls. Some may also use a similar system with permanent magnet (brush less) DC motors.

  20. In lathes, milling and grinding machines which motor will preferred?

    Lathes machines requires uniform torque. Hence squirrel cage induction motor or dc shunt motors are preferred.
