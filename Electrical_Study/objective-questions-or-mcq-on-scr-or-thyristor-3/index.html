  1. Under normal operating condition voltage clamping device offers impedance of

    Under normal condition voltage clamping device offers high impedance and acts as if it is not present in the circuit.

  2. What is used to protect the SCR from over current ?

    CB and fuse sense the high current through the circuit and breaks the circuit to protect the SCR.

  3. CB used for over current protection of thyristor operates when the fault current is

    As tripping time of CB is generally large .

  4. When a large surge current of very short duration flows through a thyristor then which one of the following device will operate to protect the thyristor ?

    FACL fuse operates very fast.

  5. How can we protect SCR from thermal conditions ?

    Excess amount of heat can be dissipated quickly by mounting the SCR on a heat sink.

  6. Which of the following is used in heat sink?

    Aluminium is a good conductor of heat. Silver is the best conductor of heat but it is not used due to its high cost.

  7. Materials used in heat sink should have

    A heat sink material should have all these characteristics to radiate heat efficiently and quickly.

  8. What is used to protect a thyristor from high di / dt conditions?

    Inductor does not allow sudden change in current through it.

  9. Which of the following is used in SCR to protect from high dv / dt?

    Capacitor in series with the resistor is called snubber circuit which does not allow sudden change in dv / dt.

  10. What happen due to high di / dt?

    High di / dt causes localized heat generation.

  11. What may happen high dv / dt?

    High dv / dt will breakdown the J2 junction and trigger the SCR.

  12. Why resistor is used in snubber circuit ?

    Capacitor discharges through the resistor. Resistor limits this discharging current.

  13. Snubber ckt is used with SCR

    Snubber circuit is used in parallel with SCR to limit high dv / dt across the SCR.

  14. Delay time is defined by the interval when

    Delay time is the interval between which gate current rises from 90 % to 100 % of its final value at the same time anode forward leakage current rises to 10 % of its final value as well as anode forward voltage falls to 90 % of its initial value.

  15. Rise time is defined by the interval when

    Rise time is known as the time interval when anode voltage drops from 90 % to 10 % of its initial value. At the same time anode current rise 10 % to 90 % of its final value.

  16. Spread time is defined as the interval during which

    Voltage across SCR never comes down to zero. There is always a ON state voltage drop present across SCR.

  17. Maximum power loss occurs during

    Only during rise time anode voltage and current simultaneously high. So power loss (V × I) is maximum here.

  18. The typical time of rise time lies between

    Generally rise time is very low around 1 – 4 μs.

  19. During which time maximum conduction spreading take place in the thyristor during turn ON

    During spread time maximum charges spread across the thyristor.

  20. During reverse recovery time

    During reverse recovery time free charge carriers swept out from the junction J1 and J3.
