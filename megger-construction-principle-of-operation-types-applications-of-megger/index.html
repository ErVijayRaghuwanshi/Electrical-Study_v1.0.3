Megger Construction Principle of Operation Types Applications of Megger

Megger is an instrument which is used as insulation tester as the resistance of the insulation is quite high. Given below is the circuit diagram of Megger.Inline image 8 As shown it consists of electric current coil which is quite similar to that of the permanent magnet moving coil instruments. The voltage Va which is thread over annular magnetic core in a weak magnetic field, at this point the pointer is at infinite position as shown in the circuit diagram. As this coil is in weak magnetic field it exerts low torque. As the voltage coil moves towards the strong magnetic field the exerted the coil will increase .when the torque exerted by the coil will reach the maximum value then the pointer will indicate the zero value. Here we have modified the value of torque by introducing another voltage coil Vb as shown on the circuit diagram. The coil Vb is so located that when it moves from weak magnetic field towards the strong magnetic field the pointer will move from infinite resistance position to zero resistance position. The resultant actions of both the voltage coils Va and Vb can be considered as though the coils constituted a spring of variable spring constant which being very stiff near the zero where electric current is very high as the resistance R is very low and the value of electric current is very small near the infinite position of the scale as the resistance R is very large which is shown in the figure. This effect open up the high resistance portion of the scale and compresses the low resistance portion of the scale. By varying the resistance and with the help of voltage selector switch we can control the range of the instrument. The resistance on the meter in mega ohms and there is also voltage scale on the meter which shows that we are measuring so and so resistance on so and so voltage.
