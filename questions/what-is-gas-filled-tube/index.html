What is gas filled tube?

An arrangement of electrodes in an insulated container containing gas constitutes a gas filled tube. Glass and ceramics are used for the insulation purposes. Technologies of both Geissler tube and vacuum pump are used for the development of this kind of tube. There is an electric discharge when there is minimum charge on the electrodes. This discharge produces light. Gas filled tube is also called as discharge tube. There are different kinds of gases that are used inside this container which contains the electrodes.

Use of Hydrogen, deuterium gases are common in this case. Also noble gases like argon, xenon, krypton, neon, helium can be used inside the insulated envelope. Elemental vapours are also used like mercury in fluorescent tubes, sodium in sodium vapour lamps, Sulphur in sulphur lamps, etc. Air, nitrogen or halogen vapours are also used for this purpose. Examples of gas filled tubes are as follows:

  1. CFL lamps, fluorescent lamps, mercury and sodium discharge lamps are gas filled lamps used for lightning.
  2. Neon lamps are low pressure gas filled tubes.
  3. Xenon flash lamps are used in cameras.
  4. Sulphur lamps also belong to this category.
