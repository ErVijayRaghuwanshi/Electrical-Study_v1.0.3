What are the different steps of thermal power generation

The total thermal generation process can be divided into two phases-

  1. Formation of steam in the boiler house.
  2. Generation of electrical power in the generator room.

Different steps of thermal power generation are listed below,

  1. First the preheated feed water is fed into boiler.
  2. Then this feed water is heated up in the boiler vessel, by the heat, produced by boiler furnace. The boiler furnace is fired by pulverized coal.
  3. As the water is heated up, steam is formed at high pressure and temperature.
  4. This steam is then passed through super heater where it further heated up.
  5. This super heated and highly pressurized steam then enters into turbine through steam NOZZLE.
  6. Here in the turbine, this steam forcefully rotates the turbine blades.
  7. Turbine blades assembly is mechanically coupled with alternator (generator) rotor shaft, hence the rotor rotates along with turbine blades assembly.
  8. As the rotor, rotates in alternator, electric power is generated, which can be fed to the power system network.
  9. The steam comes out from the turbine after doing its mechanical work for rotating turbine blades; it is fed to the condenser, where it becomes condensed in form of water.
  10. This water then again fed to the boiler via one feed water heater, as preheated feed water.

These are the primary functional steps of thermal power generation.
