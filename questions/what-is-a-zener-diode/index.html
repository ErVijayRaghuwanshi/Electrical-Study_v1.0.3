What is a Zener diode?

Conventional diodes are used to rectify the waveform, because they can concentrate the current in to the forward direction only. This property is like a door, which can open only in a particular way. If the normal diodes are connected in reverse bias mode, then it will conduct very little current. But after a predetermined safe value, if the reverse bias voltage increases then the diode is subjected to a huge current, which is called as avalanche breakdown. It can damage the diode. Zener diode is a special kind of diode which permits the flowing of current in the forward direction as well as also allows the reverse direction of current flowing. Actually the zener diodes are specially configured so that the breakdown voltages are reduced significantly. This reduced voltage is referred as zener voltage.

When a zener diode is configured in forward bias mode, it behaves like an ordinary semiconductor diode, which transmits the current in the forward direction. As the forward breakdown voltages are also reduced so this type of diodes can easily withstand comparatively higher forward bias voltage. The time when the voltage is above a particular value which called breakdown voltage or knee voltage or avalanche point. That is why zener diode is also known as avalanche diode or breakdown diode. When the zener diode is connected in reverse bias, it change the property itself, and controls the breakdown current such a way that the voltage is moreover similar like the normal zener voltage. So the zener diode is safer than ordinary diodes. They are used in small circuit as voltage reference circuit and shunt regulator. This diode is named after Clarence Zener. In several voltage stabilizer kits, zener diodes are used widely.
