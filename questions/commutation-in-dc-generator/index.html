What is commutation in dc generator?

Any DC generator consists of two winding namely field winding and armature winding. Main magnetic field is created by the field winding. When the armature rotates in the field, then a voltage is induced in the closed armature conductors. The insulated armature conductors are kept in the slots of the armature core. But the voltage generated in a coil rotating in a magnetic field is alternating in nature. The process of obtaining direct current in the load circuit is termed as commutation in dc generator. The induced current in a specific direction when the armature conductors are under the North pole. But whenever the armature conductors shifted under South pole then the induced current starts flowing in another direction. Two successive commutator segment is connected by the carbon brush and in this way whenever the winding elements connected to those segments, they become short circuited. In some other words, the change in winding element during the short circuit period is referred a commutation. The commutator is a part which is incorporated with the DC generator to perform the commutation action. The commutator is acts like a mechanical rectifier that converts the alternating current to a direct current. A commutator is made from several wedge shaped copper bars. Each segment of a commutator is insulated from each other and each segment is connected to the ends of the armature windings.

A good commutation means a sparkless commutation. To obtain the spark less commutation some special methods are adopted and they are:

  1. Resistance commutation.
  2. Voltage commutation.
  3. Compensating winding.
