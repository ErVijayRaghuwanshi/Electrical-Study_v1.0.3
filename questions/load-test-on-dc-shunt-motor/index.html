Load Test on DC Shunt Motor

There are different tests which are performed on dc machines. These tests are conducted for determining the performance of dc motors and generators. Mainly there are four types of tests – open circuit tests, load characteristic test, efficiency test and temperature rise test. Load test is also performed on both long shunt and short shunt DC motors. Load test is performed at rated load and is required for checking the commutation and the temperature rise during operation. For small motors, brake test or testing of a motor is coupled to a calibrated generator may be carried out for determination of efficiency directly. For large motors, Hopkinson’s test or back to back test is performed on the two identical dc motors for determination of the efficiency. In Hopkinson’s test two identical machines can be tested under full load conditions.

This method is economical when the large motors are to be tested on full load for longer duration. In modern testing labs, test source is from suitable motor-generator set or from thyristor supply and rectifier power supply. The motor is fitted on the test bed and coupled to a similar machine. Load test is performed on dc shunt motor to draw some performance / characteristic curves. These are – the Output power versus speed curve, output power versus current curve, output power versus torque curve and output power versus efficiency curve. Load test requires different apparatus and some procedure is to be followed which includes use of formulas for determining output power and other parameters. Different apparatus are – resistance, inductor, starter, voltmeter, ammeter, tachometer, spring balance, DPST switch, brake drum, rheostat, etc.
