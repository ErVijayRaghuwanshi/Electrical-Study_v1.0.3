What are the applications of thermistor?

Thermistor is a temperature sensing resistor. Its resistance varies with temperature. Thermistor have come from the words thermal and resistors. Thermistor is made up of semiconductors and some metallic oxides such as nickel , manganese, Iron, copper, etc. They can sense even small change in temperature. A large change in resistance takes place owing to even small variation of temperature. There are two types of thermistors. These are :

  1. PTC (positive temperature coefficient) thermistors where resistance increase with increase in temperature.
  2. NTC (negative temperature coefficient) thermistor where resistance decrease with increase in temperature.

    3. Resistance versus temperature curve shows non linear characteristic always. Thermistor best performs within a temperature range of -90 to 130 degree centigrade temperature. Thermistors are not very costly and can be easily available. They also provide quick response and are also reliable to use. Circuits using thermocouples proved millivolt outputs while thermistor circuits give high output voltages. Steinhart formulated a equation which gives the inverse of absolute temperature which is a function of resistance of the thermistor. Thermistor use are common in bridge circuits.

    Steinhart equation is given as - 1/T = A + {B×ln(R)} + {C ×(ln(R))3} 

    Where R in Ω, T in °K

    Different applications of thermistors both PTC and NTC are stated below:

    • PTC thermistors are used as timers in coil circuits of CRT displays, used as heaters in automotive industry. They are also used in current protection by acting as current limiting devices.

    • NTC themistors are used in low temperature range. They are also used in inrush current limiting devices. NTC is used in controlling temperature of incubator. Also they are used in automotive industry. NTC are commonly used in toasters, coffee makers, freezers, hair dryers, refrigerators, etc.
