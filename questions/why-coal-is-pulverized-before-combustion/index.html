Why coal is pulverized before combustion ?

Before analyzing why coal is pulverized before combustion, we need to understand what is pulverization of coal. Pulverization of coal is the process of turning coal grates into fine coal powder. This coal powder is made by crushing and grinding the coal grates. This technology came into operation from the belief that if the coal is made fine enough, it can be burnt as gas, which will increase the stability of the coal as fuel. In actual process the pulverization of coal is:

The coal is dried by hot air and then the mixture of coal and air is fed into the furnace and the combustion process takes place at a temperature around 650° F.

Another cause for using pulverized coal instead of grate coal is that, while grate coal is used, the fly ash produced is higher which mixes with the hot furnace gas and interrupts the boiler operation, but when pulverized coal is used, the amount of solid waste (slag) increases and deposited at the bottom of the boiler, but the production of fly ash is reduced and this also reduces the pollution caused by the boiler. Using pulverized coal as fuel for cyclone boilers increases the efficiency of the power plant.
