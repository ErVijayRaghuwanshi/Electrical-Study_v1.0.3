What is meant by torque?

Torque is the turning or twisting moment of a force about an axis. It is the product of the force and the perpendicular distance from the axis of the body at which the force acts.

If the force acting on a body is F and the perpendicular distance of the force from the central axis of the body is r. Then toque acting on the body is F X r Newton - meter. Think about this concept, if the distance of the force from the central axis of the body is increased, the torque will increase. That means by applying same force form more far from the centre of the body we can produce more torque. That is why it is easy to produce turning or twisting moment on a body if the force is applied as far as possible from the centre.

Now we have understood what is meant by torque. Now let’s discuss about some other physical aspects of torque. Let’s assume due to this applied force the body has rotated its one revolution. Due to this revolution, the work done is given by force × distance that is F × 2πr. Now if the body revolves at N rps then total work done in one second will be W = F × 2πr × N joules = T × 2π × N joules

Therefore, work done / sec = power = 2πNT watt.
