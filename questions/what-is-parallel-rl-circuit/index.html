What is parallel RL circuit?

Electrical circuits consists any of these three elements - Resistance (R) , inductance (L) and capacitance (C) .These elements may be connected in seires or parallel. Here we will discuss about R-L circuit . This circuit can have both series and parallel configurations.
Let the parallel R-L circuit is energised by a sinusoidal voltage. At steady state ,

IR = V / R
IL = V / j XL = V / jωL

*** Capital letters like I ,V represents r.m.s quantity , capital letters with suffix max represents maximum values and small alphabets like i, v represents instantaneous value.

However by applying Kirchoff's current Law (KCL) we get,

I = IR + IL
= V (1/ R + 1/ jωL) = VY

[ Where Y is the admittance = G + jB ; G is the conductance =1/ R Mho and B is the inductive susceptance = 1 / XL mho ]
Also the total current supplied by the source in steady state lags the voltage by impedance angle given by
θ = tan-1 -1/ωL / 1/R = tan-1 (-R / ωL)
Again, as i = iR + iL , we can further write ,
i = v /R + 1/L ∫ vdt = (Vm/ R) cosωt + (Vm/ ωL) sinωt.
