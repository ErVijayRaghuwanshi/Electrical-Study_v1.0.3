What are Filter Circuits?

Filter circuit means which is able to convert a polluted signal into a pure signal by rectifying it from the ripples. Various combinations of capacitors, inductors, and resistors are used to make different kinds of filters, but the objective is same everywhere, that is to produce a noiseless pure signal with desirable frequency range. There are two types of filter circuits which are commonly used for signal processing, they are active filter and passive filter. Passive filters are made up with passive electronic components like resistor and capacitor etc, the most basic form is termed as one pole filters.

But in active filters, additional active components like transistor, amplifier, or OpAmp are connected to boost up the signal along with filtering action. A filter is sometime used to enhance wanted signal or frequency . Filters can be classified as passive or active, analog or digital, high pass, low pass, band pass, notch, or all pass, discrete time (sampled) or continuous time, linear or non linear, infinite impulse response or finite impulse response. Proper filters are chosen by considering the area and range of application and frequency level.
