What is Watt Hour?

Watt hour is a measurement quantity, which is used as the unit of energy. In general, the energy is the product of the power and time. Watt is the unit of power, and hour is the unit of time, therefore the phrase watt hour specifically means the the amount of power consumed in a unit span of time. Watt hour is abbreviated as Wh and one watt hour means the amount of energy which is equivalent if one watt of power is consumed constantly for one hour of time span. The conversion of units is vital to generate the consumed energy data in watt hour, because the power must be in watt unit and time must be in hour unit. If the power is measured in kilowatt, then the unit of energy consumed will be Kilo Watt hour (KWh). Watt hour is very commonly used in power distribution sectors, because it gives very easy and accurate measurement of energy consumed by the consumers connected. In case of large industrial loads, the amount of consumed energy is expressed in Kilo watt hour. Think that a 75 Watt fan is running for 4 hours, and we have to measure the energy consumed by the fan in the total 4 hour time span. So, according to the formula, the energy consumed is E = PT, that is E = 75 × (4) = 300 Wh. In this way, the energy calculation is done conveniently by this method.
