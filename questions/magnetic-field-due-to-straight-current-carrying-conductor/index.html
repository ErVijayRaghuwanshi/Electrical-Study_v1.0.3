Magnetic field due to straight current carrying conductor.

In the figure it is shown that a infinitely long straight conductor is carrying a current i in the upward direction. The magnetic field of such a conductor consists of circular lines of force having their plane perpendicular to the conductor and their centres at the centre of the conductor. The direction of line of force can be determined by right hand rule.

Let the field at a point where a N - pole is placed as shown in the figure, is H and the distance of that point from the centre of the conductor is r. Field strength at that point is H - means the N-pole wile experience a force of H Newton at that point. The direction of this force will be tangential to the circular force line. Now if the N pole is moved one round the conductor against this force, then work done is = Force × distance.

W = H × 2πr Joules ........................(i)

Again it can be proved that when a unit N-pole takes a round of a current carrying conductor, the work done is numerically equal to the current flowing through the conductor. That means

W = i Joules ...........................(ii)

From, equation (i) and (ii) we get,

H × 2πr = i
