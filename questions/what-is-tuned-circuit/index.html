What is tuned circuit?

Electrical circuits are made up of different circuit elements like inductor, resistor, capacitor, etc. An electrical circuit is called tuned circuit if it consists of inductance and capacitance i.e LC circuit. Tuned circuits are also called as resonant circuit or tank circuits. Tuned circuits are used mostly in filters, frequency mixers, etc. It also finds its applications in tuners and oscillators. This type of circuit is used for generating or picking up of signal at a particular frequency.

There are two basic types of tuned circuits â€“ series and parallel. When the tuned circuit oscillates at its resonant frequency it can store electrical energy. Here both the capacitor and inductor stores the energy. Inductor L stores it in the magnetic field while capacitor stores energy in between its plates. How does a tuned circuit operates?

When we connect a charged capacitor is connected across an inductor, charges flow through inductor. This sets up a magnetic field around it and thus minimizing voltage across capacitor. In this all charge will dissipate and voltage across capacitor is zero. Current flow does not stops because inductor inhibits current changes. Magnetic field will start to reduce much. This current is going to charge the capacitor with opposite polarity voltage. When magnetic field totally vanishes current flow stops and charge stores inside capacitor again. In this cyclic manner operation of tuned circuit occurs. In tuned circuits oscillations are very fast. Energy moves to and fro through the inductor.
