What is the direction of magnetic flux ?

In electromagnetism, magnetic flux is a measure of the magnetization of any material. The term magnetic flux refers to the number of magnetic lines of force passing through a unit area of a magnetic field. The SI unit of the magnetic flux is Weber. Sometimes, determination of the direction of magnetic flux is necessary for analyzing the magnetic field or for any other calculation. The direction of magnetic flux can be easily determined by using either Fleming's right hand rule or by the well known corkscrew rule.

Let have some discussion about the application of the above said rules.

• Right-hand rule: Consider, you have a conductor wire & a light bulb & a voltage source. If you connect them in a series connection then an amount of current will flow through the conductor & a magnetic field also developed surrounding the conductor. Then, if you hold the conductor by your right hand in such a manner that your thumb points to the direction of the current, then rest of your fingers will show the actual direction of the magnetic flux.

• Corkscrew rule: Imagine you have a simple corkscrew & with the same circuit arrangement and you have to determine the direction of the magnetic flux. So, you have to place the corkscrew such a way that the direction of current is pointed by it. Then start to rotate the corkscrew in the forward direction & just think that you are fitting the corkscrew in the wall. Automatically, the movement of that corkscrew will indicate the direction of the magnetic field.
