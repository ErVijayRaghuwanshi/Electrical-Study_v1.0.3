What is the voltmeter circuit?

Voltmeter is an important device for measuring electric potential difference, or in simple words DC or AC voltage. Generally, voltmeters are used to measure the voltage upto a certain level. High voltage measurements are also done by voltmeter, but additionally an instrument transformer is used for those situations. There are two types of voltmeter, namely analog and digital type. The voltmeter circuit mechanism is different for both of them, but the digital voltmeter uses more sophisticated electronic circuits. So, the output of digital voltmeter is more accurate and reliable.

The basic circuit of an analog voltmeter is inspired from the concept of moving coil type galvanometer. A small coil made with thin copper wire is placed inside a magnetic field. The two terminals of the copper coil is used as the probes. That is voltage to be measured is applied at these two terminals. A pointer is attached with the voltage coil. This pointer is uniformly projected upon a scale. Whenever a potential difference or voltage is applied on the copper coil, current starts flowing through it. So, a magnetic field develops around the coil. This magnetic field interacts with the stationary magnetic field. This interaction generates a torque, which leads to rotate the coil along with the pointer upon the scale. In this way, the voltage is measured in analog type voltmeter. Analog to digital converter is used additionally in case of digital type voltmeter.
