Magnetic field due to a current in a solenoid

Assume one solenoid having N number of turns and length of L meter. Also assume the field strength inside the solenoid is H and it is constant through out the length L. Suppose a unit N pole is placed at an point A and is taken once along the path, shown in the figure.

If this pole travels along the path the total work done will be

W = H × L = HL Joules................(i)

Since, the force H acts only over the Length L inside the solenoid.

Again, as the unit N pole is taken trough the solenoid, its flux of 1 Wb is cut by every turn of the solenoid. As the solenoid is carrying a current of I Amp, every turn of the solenoid carries the same current I Amp. Hence, work done in that respect will be
W = N × I = NI Joules.................(ii)

Equating (i) and (ii), we get,

HL = NI or H = NI/L Amp - turns / meter

Again, we know that B = μ0.μr.H Wb/m2

Hence, flux density is given by

B = μ0.μr.NI/L Wb/m2 in any medium

For air, B = μ0.NI/L Wb/m2 Since Î¼r = 1 in air.
