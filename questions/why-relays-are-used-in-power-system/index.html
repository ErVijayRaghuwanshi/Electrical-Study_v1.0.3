Why relays are used in Power System?

Electrical power system deals with generation, transmission and distribution of various voltage levels and it works on a very big network. So proper precautionary level must be adopted to prevent accidents and save both human and costly appliances. Basically, the expense related to the power system (installing, maintenance etc.) is quite high. So this also increases the need of taking adequate safety measures.

Various measures are taken to increase the safety of a power system network. Because a huge flow of current in any circuit can damage the costly equipments connected with it. Relay and circuit breaker combination is the most widely used protective measure. The main operation of relay is to sense the excess flow of current and trip the circuit breaker coil which disconnects the faulty part of the network from the healthy part, so that the huge current flow does not affect the equipment of that healthy area. The relays not only trips the circuit breaker when huge current flows through the system, but also they are used to detect various abnormal condition of the system by alarming. There are some special type of relays which are called non trip relay as they are only used for alarming purposes. In addition to that there are some tripping relays which are not actuated by the over current in the system, but by over voltage, under voltage, over frequency, under frequency, as these are also abnormal conditions of the system. Also there are various mechanical relays used in electrical power system, which are actuated by the thrust of insulating oil (Buchholz Relay in power transformer), SF6 gas pressure (density monitor in SF6 circuit breaker), or air pressure (in pneumatic circuit breaker)etc. But whatever may be the type of relays, each of them are installed in electrical power system to prevent abnormalities in the system.
