What is ideal diode?

Diode is an electronic device which converges the electric current in one direction, in the forward direction. An Ideal diode is such kind of diode which has the ideal characteristics. Practically, the performance of the real diodes slightly differs from that of the ideal diodes. As the current in a diodes is directed only in the forward direction, so the forward bias resistance (that is the resistance lumped in forward direction of diode, or the resistance which is subjected to the forward bias current). So the forward bias resistance is null in case of ideal diodes, but in practical situations, real diodes posses very few forward bias resistance. The current does not have any return path, so an ideal diode have infinite reverse bias resistance. Therefore, in reverse bias operation, it acts like an open circuit. The total voltage drop across an ideal diode is zero so it acts like a conductor, which carries the entire amount of current into the forward direction. But in case of real diodes there will little voltage drop across the diode which is termed as forward voltage drop. For Germanium diodes it is 0.3 V and for silicon diodes it is 0.6 V, this few voltage is considered during the calculation with real diodes. Practically ideal diode representation is impossible because of its physical limitations. But real diodes are manufactured such a way, that the characteristics are almost similar with the ideal one. In practice, real diodes are seen in several electronic circuits like filter circuit.
