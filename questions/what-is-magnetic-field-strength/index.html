What is magnetic field strength?

Magnetic field strength at any point within a magnetic field is numerically equal to the force experienced by a N-pole of one weber placed at that point. Unit of magnetic firld strength is Newton per weber i.e N/Wb. Suppose we require to find the field intensity at a point A which is r metre from a pole of m webers. Imagine a similar pole of one weber placed at point A. The force experience by this pole is given by ,

F = m × 1 / (4 π μ0 r2) Newton.

Therefore, magnetic field intensity H can be written as , H = m / 4πμ0 r3 N/Wb or (A/m) or Oersted. Also if a pole of m Wb is place in a uniform field of strength H N/Wb, then force experienced by the pole is equal to mH Newtons. It should be noted that the field strength is a vector quantity having both magnitude and direction.
