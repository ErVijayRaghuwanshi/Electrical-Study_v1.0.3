What is insulator and define insulator?

Depending upon the tendency of allowing current to flow through a material, there are three types of material,

(i) Conductor.

(ii) Semiconductor.

(iii) Insulator.

The presence of the free electrons determines the character of the material. Conductors, through which current flows freely, have many free electrons and that's why conductors are used to make the wires for transmission lines. In conducting wires current can freely pass and this is the perfect phenomenon for a good conductor.

On the other hand insulators are those materials which donâ€™t have much free electrons and for that reason, current cannot move freely through them.

And finally semiconductors are those materials which have less free electrons than conductors but more free electrons than insulators.

So, in general insulators can be defined as the objects which can oppose the flow of heat, electricity, light or sound through it.

Some examples of insulating materials are air, cloth, rubber etc.

Main usage of insulators are in transmission sector. Wires are hung from towers via insulators, these insulators resist the flow of current through the towers. Switches are made of insulating materials, wires used for distribution purpose are covered by insulating materials for the safety reasons.
