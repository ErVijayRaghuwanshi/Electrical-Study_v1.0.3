What is electrical power?

Apparently we are used to the terms like voltage, current, resistance, impedance. We know that at generation plant electricity is generated. Through the transmission lines electricity is transmitted. At electrical substitutions the high voltage is converted into low voltage then this low voltage is delivered to common consumers. But in all these processes the main component is electrical power or we can say electrical energy. Electrical power is generated at power stations, power is transferred through transmission lines. Power is delivered to consumers at distribution end. So, the main component of electrical power system is the power. Power is nothing but the flow of energy per unit time, it is also represented as the product of voltage and current flow.
P = V x I. the unit of power is Watt, which is Joules/second, i.e rate of energy flow per second. Power can be defined by the following forms also. P = I2.R = V2 / R.
For AC circuits power can be divided into three types:
Real power
Reactive power
Apparent power

Real power is the actual or usable power of the line, which does the required works
P= Vrms Irms Cos θ
Where P = Real power in watts
Vrms = rms voltage = Vpeak / √2
θ = phase angle.
Similarly, reactive power is the unused power of the network which is represented by
Q = Vrms Irms Sin θ
Where Q = Reactive power
Apparent power is the total power
S = Vrms Irms
So, P2 + Q2 = S2 and the power triangle helps to understand the interconnection of these 3 types of power very easily.
