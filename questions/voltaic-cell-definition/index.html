Voltaic cell definition

We can convert chemical energy to electrical energy with the help of a voltaic cell. This voltaic cell is also called primary cell in which a pair of electrodes made up of dissimilar metals are immersed in a solution. This solution undergoes chemical reaction or change to produce a voltage.

A voltaic cell is thus an electrochemical cell which produces electrical energy using chemical reaction. In this process redox reaction that is oxidation –reduction is involved. The voltaic cell is sometimes also called as Galvanic cell named after Luigi Galvani. Alessandro Volta , Italian scientist invented the voltaic cell. Here two metal electrodes immersed in a electrolytic solution is connected externally by a circuit. One metal is reduced i.e gain electrons while other is oxidized i.e loose electrons. The oxidation potential of two metal is different and this difference provides the electric power.

A voltaic cell consists of following components. They are:
· Anode – electrode where oxidation occurs
· Cathode – electrode where reduction occurs
· Salt bridge – electrolyte chamber which completes the circuit of the cell
· External circuit – provides a path for flow of electrons between electrodes
· Load – present in the external circuit which utilizes a part of the electrons to perform its function (ex- an electric bulb can be made to glow)
· Half cell.
