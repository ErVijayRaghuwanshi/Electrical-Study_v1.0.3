How DC Generator works ?

The electrical machines which generates dc electricity is called dc generator. The basic diagram or the main parts of a dc generator is given below.

In case of the dc generator mechanical energy is converted into electrical energy. Prime mover moves the rotor of the generator which is present in the magnetic field of a permanent magnet or electric field. Now, we know that if a coil completed circuit is made to rotate in a magnetic field then there is a current flow in the circuit, which is the main operating principle of a dc generator. The stator part of the generator supplies the current.

Now, if Ea is generated voltage and Iara is the armature loss and Vt is the terminal voltage then the relation between all these can be written as

Ea = Vt + Iara

Now depending on the position of field there are three types of dc generators.

  1. Series- field in the series with the rotor
  2. Shunt – field is in parallel with the rotor
  3. Compound – both series and parallel field are present in the generator

The main difference between dc generator and dc motor is that in case of dc generator mechanical energy is converted into electrical energy and in case of dc motor electrical energy is converted into mechanical energy, so both of them can be said as the mirror image of one another. Their working principle is also similar. This is how DC generator works.
