What are the applications of oscillator?

Oscillators are devices that are used to generate repetitive signals. They produce output signals without an input signal. There are two major types of electronic oscillators: harmonic oscillators and relaxation oscillators. Harmonic oscillators produce sine wave outputs. Relaxation oscillators produce non-sine wave outputs such as square wave, rectangular wave, and sawtooth outputs. Oscillation is the periodic variation, typically in time, of some measure as seen, for example, in a swinging pendulum. The term vibration is sometimes used more narrowly to mean a mechanical oscillation but sometimes is used to be synonymous with oscillation.

An oscillator can be divided into three definite sections:

an amplifier

the feedback connections

the frequency determining components.

An oscillator is a circuit which produces a continuous output signal; thus it is called a signal generator. When the signal produced is a sine wave of constant amplitude and frequency, the oscillator circuit is called a sine wave generator. The oscillator can produce a square wave signal in digital logic families such as TTL, CMOS, or ECL.

  • Advantages of oscillators:-
  1. Operate at or near series resonance (about 5 ppm to 40 ppm
    from Fs).
  2. Very good short-term stability.
  3. Work at any frequency from 1 kHz to 200 MHz.
  4. Circuit provides a large output signal.
  5. Drives the crystal at a low power level.
  6. Oscillation frequency almost insensitive to small changes in the series resistance or shunt capacitance.
