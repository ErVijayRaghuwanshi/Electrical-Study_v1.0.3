What are Switching Diodes?

Switching diodes or pulse diodes are specially designed semiconductor diodes having a switching time of less than 1 micro second. These diodes are used in high speed or fast response circuits. A p-n junction diode can act as a switch. It is in ON state that is conducting when forward biased and in OFF state (non conducting) when reverse biased. The change over from one state to another is accompanied by a transient. A certain amount of time elapses when diode is switched from the reverse bias to forward bias state. This time is known as the forward recovery time (tfr) which is defined as the time gap between which diode voltage attains 10% of its final value and diode voltage reaches 90% of its final value. Reverse recovery time is also prevalent.
