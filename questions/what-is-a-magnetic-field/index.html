What is a magnetic field

The magnetic field refers to the surrounding area of a magnet where the magnetic influence is activated. The magnetic field is not smoothly distributed along the magnet. In the pole areas, that is in North pole and South pole the field is highly focused. In this way, a domain is formed surrounding a magnet where the attraction or repulsion property of that magnet is activated.

We know, magnetic lines of force originated from the North pole & after passing through the surrounding air ultimately enters into the South pole and then travel through the magnet towards North pole. In this way a complete path is created. Actually, this imaginary lines gives the actual pattern of the magnetic field. The more density of magnetic lines of force leads more strong magnetic field. This magnetic field is invisible but the presence of it can be identified by some special device. If you have two bar magnets then you will observe that any two unlike poles are attracted by each other and like poles are repulsed by each other and you should feel the force. This force is developed by the magnetic field.

The magnetic field of any electromagnet is depended on the amount of current flowing through it. This relationship with electricity is the main working principle of several electromagnetically operated power tools. You should know that our earth has its own magnetic field and it is called as magnetosphere and this field helps to screen out the harmful cosmic radiation coming from the universe.
