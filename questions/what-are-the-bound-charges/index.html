What are the bound charges?

Electric polarization of a material produces bound charges. We come across the term electric polarization in classical electromagnetism. Bound charges are involved with electric polarization. Let us discuss what is electric polarization?

Electric polarization or polarization density or simply polarization is the measure of density of electric dipole moment in a dielectric material. Polarization is a vector quantity. It is always represented by P. In dielectric material there are two charges free and bound charges. In response to an applied electric field electric dipoles are set up by the bound charges. Bound charges are named so because they are bound that is they cannot be removed.

In dielectric material charges are bound to the nuclei. Bound surface charge is defined as the charge accumulated on the surface of the dielectric. In a polarized object bound charges originate from alignment of polar molecules. In a polarized object electric dipoles are aligned. All dipoles if aligned then the charges will cancel out each other.

Positive charge will cancel out a negative charge. There is no charge cancellation possible on the surface of the object. Free charges are those which existed before the polarization took place. Total charge density comes from both free and bound charges. A conductor consists of both kinds of charges - free and bound. Structure of atom gives us the idea of free and bound charges in a conductor. Bound charges here are the fixed charges that is fixed to the lattice and are positive ions.
