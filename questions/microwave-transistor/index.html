Microwave transistor

Microwave transistors are useful as small-signal amplifiers to 6 GHz and power amplifiers to 4 GHz. Nearly all microwave transistors are of the silicon planar type. Power transistors use three types of geometries--interdigitated, overlay, and mesh-while small-signal transistors use interdigatated only. The general theory of the frequency response of transistors is reviewed, including active and inactive elements. A condensed description of the design and processing steps for a silicon microwave transistor is given.

A final section deals with the types of high-frequency measurements used in the design and analysis of transistors.The microwave band of frequencies in the 300 MHz to 300 GHz electromagnetic spectrum. According to the functional classification, microwave transistor comprises a microwave low noise transistor and microwave power transistor. Classification by structure, microwave transistor can be divided into bipolar transistor and field-effect transistor. Due to the high operating frequency, fine geometric size of microwave transistor must have a micron or sub-micron.

They are Classified as:

Microwave power transistor- the transistor has good power gain and efficiency at microwave frequencies. High frequency and high power are contradictory, the microwave power transistor design shall be considered from the aspects of the device structure, physical parameters, electrical and thermal conductivity. Main ways to increase the frequency, power performance are: improve the emitter "perimeter / area ratio", in order to improve the unit emission current capacity extremely.

Microwave low noise transistor- it is used for receiver pre-amplifier microwave communication, satellite communication, radar, electronic countermeasure and telemetry, remote control system. Microwave transistor noise is low, the sensitivity of the receiver is higher, the role of these systems the greater distance.
