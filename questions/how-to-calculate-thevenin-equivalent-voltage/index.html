How to calculate thevenin equivalent voltage?

Thevenin’s theorem is a simplification technique used in circuit analysis. Any complex network with several voltage sources & resistors can be reduced to a Thevenin’s equivalent circuit consist of a single voltage source and a series resistance connected to a load.

The Thevenin's equivalent voltage is determined by the following steps:

• To measure the Thevenin's equivalent voltage, remove the load resistance from the circuit.

• Then measure the open circuit voltage appears across the terminals of that load, this open circuit voltage is called Thevenin's equivalent voltage.

Determination of Thevenin's equivalent voltage is an important step as this voltage is required to calculate the branch current.
