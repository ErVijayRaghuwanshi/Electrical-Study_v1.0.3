What is pulverized coal combustion ?

Pulverized Coal Combustion method or PCC method is the most common method which used in modern day power plants. The main cause of adopting this method of combustion is that, the overall efficiency of a power plant increases radically if the fuel used is of high quality and less volatile. Pulverized coal is produced by crushing the coal grates and turning them into finely ground coal particles, so that less than 2% is 300 m and 70-75% is below 75 m.

During pulverized coal combustion, this finely grounded coal particles are injected into the furnace. After mixing with preheated air, the particles are ignited by the heat and the operation of the furnace starts. After the combustion, large amount of solid waste is produced which is disposed of by the running water at the bottom of the boiler. The remaining mixture of pulverized coal and air is mixed with newly introduced tertiary air and again the combustion process initiates.

The boilers using pulverized coal as fuel, operates at very high pressure and temperature and boosts the efficiency of the power plants very much. Another good advantage of pulverized coal combustion (PCC) is the production of less fly ash and more solid waste, which results in reduced pollutants.
