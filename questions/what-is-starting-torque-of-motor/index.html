What is Starting Torque of Motor?

Motor essentially consists of two parts â€“ stator and the rotor. Stator is the stationary part which is fixed and rotor is the rotating part. Torque is an important parameter which is required for rotating the rotor of the motor. Starting torque is the torque which is produced at the beginning of starting of motor that is when the motor is at rest or standstill. If the start load is low, low starting torque is desirable but we need high starting torque for motors with large loads such as pumps, cranes, etc. Low loads include centrifugal pump or fans. Starting torque of motor is also called as locked rotor torque.

In 3 phase induction motor, slip is unity when the rotor is at standstill condition (since slip = Ns - Nr / Ns). So we get starting torque by putting slip equal to one in the torque equation of an induction motor. Starting torque expression reveals that it is directly proportional to the square of the voltage applied to stator. Starting torque also reaches its maximum value and it is obtained when rotor resistance equals to the total rotor reactance. For increasing the starting torque an additional resistance is to be added to rotor circuit at start. Here the rotor resistance is not more than 1-2 % of leakage reactance for high efficiency.
