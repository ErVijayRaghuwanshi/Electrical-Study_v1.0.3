What is microwave frequency range?

Microwaves are a form of electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths ranging from as long as one meter to as short as one millimetre, or equivalently, with frequencies between 300 MHz (0.3 GHz) and 300 GHz. This broad definition includes both UHF and EHF (millimetre waves), and various sources use different boundaries. In all cases, microwave includes the entire SHF band (3 to 30 GHz, or 10 to 1 cm) at minimum, with RF engineering often putting the lower boundary at 1 GHz (30 cm), and the upper around 100 GHz (3 mm).

Mainly used in Airborne radar, microwave links, common- carrier land mobile communication, satellite communication etc. Before the introduction of Faber optic transmission, Microwave was used in Telephone systems for long distance communication. In 1950, the Frequency division multiplex system was used to transfer up to 5400 channels of telephone signals via Microwave. Now Microwave is extensively used in Satellite communication, Mobile phones, Bluetooth devices, Wi-Fi systems etc. Cable TV and Internet access through Coaxial cables use low power Microwave frequencies. Microwave also finds applications in Remote sensing, Radar detection, Motion detection, Radio astronomy, Microwave imaging in Bio-medicine etc.
