Moving coil meter

Moving coil meter is a device in which the working principle is the application of force on a coil placed in a magnetic field. A common use of the forces on a coil in a magnetic field is that of the moving coil meter. The coil is suspended between the poles of a magnet on jewelled bearings and is held in place by two finely coiled springs through which the current to be measured passes in and out of the coil. We also see the pole pieces are shaped so that the magnetic field is radial thus giving the maximum and constant torque on the coil whatever its position. There is a soft iron armature in the centre of the coil, and this further concentrates the magnetic field through it due to the high value of the relative permeability of the material of the core. The coil is supported on a light metal frame and induced currents in this frame give electromagnetic damping of the movement. If you look carefully at the constructional diagram then you will see that how the copper coil is moving under two poles. When it is in equilibrium with a current passing through it, the torque on the coil produced by the magnetic field is balanced by an opposing torque due to the rigidity of the springs. Clearly the more delicate the springs the bigger the deflection for a given current.

Some meters are of the suspended coil type; the coil is mounted on a fine phosphor bronze wire and the deflection measured with a lamp and scale. A small mirror mounted above the coil reflects a beam of light on to a scale, the angle of twist of the light beam being double the angle of rotation of the coil. Such instruments have a framework of brass or aluminium. It is often we see that meters are made with a protective series resistor since too large a current will burn out the springs or the coil. When the correct range for the meter has been found this protective resistor may be shorted out. Now a days we are truly accustomed with the digital meters rather than these moving coil analogue meter which is used less and less these days.
