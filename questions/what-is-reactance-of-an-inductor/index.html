What is reactance of an inductor?

Let an a.c source of voltage v volt is applied to a pure inductor. Inductance of the inductor is L henry and instantaneous current is i amps. Instantaneous current i can be expressed as, i = Im sinωt where Im is the maximum current .

Induced voltage e is given as , e = L di / dt

= L d / dt ( Im sinωt )

= ωL Im cosωt

= Vm cosωt (when ωL Im is equal to Vm, it indicates peak value of induced voltage ).

e = Vm sin( ωt + 90 ) ................... (i)

From equn. (i) we get two things,

Vm = ωL Im giving Vrms = ωL Irms i.e equal to XLI where (XL= ωL ). This term XL is termed as inductive reactance / reactance of an inductor . This is measured in ohms Ω. The curremt through inductor lags the voltage across inductor by 90°.
