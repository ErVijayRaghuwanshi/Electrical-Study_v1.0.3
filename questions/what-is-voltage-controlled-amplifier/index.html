What is Voltage Controlled Amplifier?

A voltage controlled amplifier is used in many fields in electronics, and also termed as VCA. Amplifiers are used in the circuits to boost the energy level or the signal level. The gain of an amplifier is simply the output versus input ratio, which indicates the performance of the amplifier. VCA is a special type of controlled amplifier, controlled in the sense of controlling the overall gain of the amplifier. In general two quadrant and four quadrant operations are commonly used for VCA circuits. A voltage controlled amplifier is controlled by a control voltage signal, which is provided from an another source. The value or the amplitude of the control signal (that is the voltage) determines the gain of the voltage controlled amplifier.

There are several uncontrolled amplifier, where the gain is constant for certain range of operation. But, there are several methods of gain control, in order to achieve more sophisticated control of electronics signals and waves. Somewhere, resistance controlled methods is used, but VCA is used several special applications, as the voltage controlled amplifier circuit provides more flexibility for the operator. The control signal is voltage, so the voltage control is more easier for the operator. The gain variation is also very definite for a selected range of operation. Voltage controlled amplifier circuits are mostly used for wave modulation purposes.
